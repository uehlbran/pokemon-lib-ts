# Generation 5 Battle Mechanics Spec

## Quick Start

Gen 5 is the **generation of new mechanics and quality-of-life features**. The three major changes are:

1. **Hidden Abilities (Dream World slot)**: Each Pokémon gains a third ability slot called the Hidden Ability, available from Dream World encounters or specific in-game events. This mechanic becomes crucial for competitive play.

2. **New Stat/Status mechanics**: Paralysis speed penalty remains ×0.25 (same as Gen 3–4; the change to ×0.5 happens in Gen 7). Sleep duration shortens to 1–3 turns (from Gen 3's 2–5). Explosion and Self-Destruct lose their Defense-halving effect.

3. **Triple and Rotation Battles**: New battle formats (3v3 with positional mechanics or 3-on-1 rotating).

Other Gen 5 introductions:

- **Team Preview**: Players see opponent's full team before battle starts (no switching in)
- **41 new abilities**, including game-changers: Prankster, Magic Bounce, Regenerator, Multiscale, Sheer Force, Sand Rush, Sand Force, Moxie, Defiant, Justified, Contrary, Weak Armor, Overcoat, Harvest, Moody, Cursed Body, Illusion, Imposter, Zen Mode, Mummy, Analytic, Victory Star, Unburden (expanded), Telepathy
- **Sturdy rework**: Now blocks OHKO moves AND survives 1-HP OHKO moves (like Focus Sash)
- **ExtremeSpeed priority +2** (changed from Gen 4's +1)
- **Damage formula modifier chaining**: Gen 5 uses 4096-based modifier stacking with pokeRound
- **New field effects**: Magic Room, Wonder Room, Quick Guard, Wide Guard
- **Gems**: 1.5x single-use damage items for each type (17 total)
- **New held items**: Eviolite, Rocky Helmet, Air Balloon, Red Card, Eject Button, Ring Target, Absorb Bulb, Cell Battery, Black Sludge
- **Tailwind 4 turns** (changed from Gen 4's 3)
- **Encore exactly 3 turns** (simplified from Gen 4's variable range)
- **Taunt 3 turns** (simplified from Gen 4's variable range)
- **Growth in Sun: +2 Attack and +2 SpAtk** (changed from Gen 4's +1/+1)
- **Knock Off base 20 power, NO damage boost** (1.5x boost is Gen 6+)
- **EXP formula completely rewritten** with new scaling system
- **Pledge moves (Fire/Grass/Water Pledge)**: 50 base power individually, 150 combined in doubles with field effects
- **Weak Armor: -1 Defense, +1 Speed** on physical contact (NOT +2 Speed — changed in Gen 7)

**What does NOT exist in Gen 5** (commonly confused — LLM training data is biased toward Gen 6+ mechanics):

- No Fairy type (Gen 6) — type chart is still 17 types
- No permanent weather nerf (Gen 6) — ability weather (Drizzle, Drought, Sand Stream, Snow Warning) is STILL INDEFINITE
- No Terrain (Gen 6+ for competitive impact; grassy/electric/misty/psychic terrain are Gen 7+)
- No Mega Evolution (Gen 6)
- No 1.5x crit multiplier (Gen 6) — still 2.0x in Gen 5
- No Knock Off damage boost (Gen 6+) — still base 20 in Gen 5
- No Assault Vest (Gen 6)
- No Ability Capsule (Gen 6)
- No Pixilate/Aerilate/Refrigerate (Gen 6)
- No Gale Wings priority block (Gen 6)
- Paralysis is ×0.25 Speed (unchanged from Gen 3–4; ×0.5 is Gen 7)
- Weak Armor is -1 Def/+1 Speed (NOT +2 Speed — changed in Gen 7)
- Overcoat only blocks weather damage (NOT powder moves — added in Gen 6)
- Moody CAN affect accuracy/evasion (Gen 8 removed this ability)
- Prankster is NOT blocked by Dark-type moves (Gen 7 added this restriction)
- Burn is 1/8 max HP (NOT 1/16 — changed in Gen 7)
- Protean/Stance Change do NOT exist (Gen 6+)

**Gen 6+ abilities that do NOT exist in Gen 5** (do not implement these):

Pixilate, Aerilate, Refrigerate, Mega Launcher, Tough Claws, Parental Bond, Dark Aura, Fairy Aura, Aura Break, Gale Wings (priority blocking), Bulletproof, Fur Coat, Magician, Cheek Pouch, Symbiosis, Stance Change, Sweet Veil, Flower Veil, Grass Pelt, Competitive, Protean

## 1. Stat System

### Formula

The HP and stat formulas are **identical to Gen 3 and Gen 4**. No changes in Gen 5.

**HP formula:**
```
HP = floor(((2 * Base + IV + floor(EV / 4)) * Level) / 100) + Level + 10
```

**Non-HP stat formula:**
```
stat = floor((floor(((2 * Base + IV + floor(EV / 4)) * Level) / 100) + 5) * NatureMod)
```

Where:
- **Base**: Pokémon's base stat (0–255)
- **IV**: Individual value (0–31)
- **EV**: Effort value (0–255 per stat, 510 total)
- **Level**: Pokémon's level (1–100)
- **NatureMod**: Nature multiplier (1.1 = +10%, 0.9 = −10%, 1.0 = neutral)

### Shedinja Exception

Shedinja always has 1 HP, regardless of stats.

### Natures

25 natures, unchanged from Gen 3–4:
- 6 neutral natures: Hardy, Docile, Serious, Bashful, Quirky
- 20 boosting natures: 5 Atk modifiers × 4 stat reductions each

Each nature boosts one stat by +10% and reduces another by −10%.

### Example Verification

**Level 50 Charizard with 31 HP IV, 252 HP EV, neutral nature (Hardy):**
```
Base = 78
IV = 31
EV = 252
Level = 50

HP = floor(((2 * 78 + 31 + floor(252 / 4)) * 50) / 100) + 50 + 10
   = floor(((156 + 31 + 63) * 50) / 100) + 60
   = floor((250 * 50) / 100) + 60
   = floor(125) + 60
   = 185
```

Expected from Bulbapedia: **185 HP** ✓

## 3. Damage Formula

### Base Formula

The damage formula is **identical to Gen 3–4**. No changes in Gen 5.

```
levelFactor = floor(2 * Level / 5) + 2
baseDamage = floor(floor(levelFactor * Power * A / D) / 50) + 2
```

Where:
- **levelFactor**: Depends on user's level
- **Power**: Move's base power (0–250)
- **A**: User's relevant attacking stat (Atk or SpAtk) after modifiers
- **D**: Opponent's relevant defensive stat (Def or SpDef) after modifiers

### Critical Hit and Type Effectiveness

After baseDamage is calculated:
1. **Critical hit**: multiply by 2.0x if critical (unchanged from Gen 3–4; becomes 1.5x in Gen 6)
2. **Type effectiveness**: multiply by 2x, 0.5x, 0x, or 1x
3. **Other multipliers**: Apply in sequence

### Modifier Chaining (Gen 5 specific)

Gen 5 uses **4096-based modifier chaining** with pokeRound precision:

```
result = floor((value * modifier + 2048) / 4096)
```

This replaces Gen 3–4's looser decimal chaining. Each modifier is applied sequentially to the result of the previous calculation.

**Example**: Stab (1.5x) × Weather (0.5x) applied to base 100 damage:
```
Step 1: floor((100 * 1.5 * 4096 + 2048) / 4096) = floor(614400 + 2048) / 4096 = floor(150.5) = 150
Step 2: floor((150 * 0.5 * 4096 + 2048) / 4096) = floor(307200 + 2048) / 4096 = floor(75.5) = 75
```

### Spread Move Penalty

Spread moves (target multiple foes) apply 0.75x damage. This is **unchanged from Gen 4**.

Examples: Earthquake (in doubles), Surf (in doubles), Explosion (in doubles/triples).

### Abilities Affecting Damage

Several Gen 5 abilities modify damage:

- **Sheer Force**: 1.3x damage to moves with secondary effects; secondary effect is removed when this activates. Life Orb recoil is skipped.
- **Analytic**: 1.3x damage if moving last in turn order
- **Sand Force**: 1.3x damage to Rock/Ground/Steel moves in sandstorm
- **Regenerator, Magic Bounce, Prankster**: Do NOT affect base damage (see Abilities section)

### Items Affecting Damage

- **Life Orb**: 1.3x damage, 1/10 recoil
- **Gems** (Fire Gem, Water Gem, etc.): 1.5x damage to move of matching type; single use, consumed after move executes
- **Choice Scarf/Specs**: 1.5x for Speed/SpAtk respectively; locks move choice
- **Rocky Helmet, Eviolite, Black Sludge**: See Items section

## 5. Abilities

Gen 5 introduces **41 new abilities** (160+ total). The most game-changing are:

### Dream World Abilities (Third Slot)

Each Pokémon has a potential third ability called the **Hidden Ability** (or Dream World ability). This slot is only available in Gen 5 from Dream World encounters or specific in-game events.

**Example hidden abilities:**
- Bulbasaur: Overgrow (normal) → Chlorophyll (hidden)
- Charmander: Blaze (normal) → Solar Power (hidden)
- Ditto: Imposter (hidden — only slot in Gen 5)

### Key Gen 5 Abilities

#### Prankster
**Effect**: User's status moves have +1 priority.

Does NOT include moves with priority from other sources (e.g., Tail Glow is not a status move even if priority-adjusted).

**Interaction**: In Gen 5, Prankster is NOT blocked by Dark-type opponents (Gen 7 adds this restriction).

#### Magic Bounce
**Effect**: Reflects moves with no direct damage (Stealth Rock, Reflect, Light Screen, Spikes, Toxic Spikes, Thunder Wave, Will-O-Wisp, Leech Seed, etc.) back to the user.

Same reflection list as the move **Magic Coat**.

#### Regenerator
**Effect**: Heals 1/3 max HP when switched out.

Activates even if switched out by opponent's move (e.g., Dragon Tail).

#### Multiscale
**Effect**: Reduces damage to user by 50% when at full HP. Breaks and remains broken after damage.

**Interaction**: If user heals back to full HP (e.g., Roost + another healing source), Multiscale reforms.

#### Sheer Force
**Effect**: User's moves with secondary effects gain 1.3x damage; secondary effect is suppressed.

**Life Orb interaction**: If Sheer Force is active, Life Orb recoil does NOT apply.

**Affected moves**: Hundreds of moves; essentially any with secondary chance > 0%.

#### Sand Rush
**Effect**: Speed is doubled in sandstorm. User is immune to sandstorm damage.

#### Sand Force
**Effect**: Rock/Ground/Steel moves deal 1.3x damage in sandstorm. User is immune to sandstorm damage.

#### Moxie
**Effect**: +1 Attack when user's move KOs opponent.

Triggers after opponent faints, even if user faints same turn (e.g., from entry hazards/burn).

#### Defiant
**Effect**: +2 Attack when user's stat is lowered by opponent's move.

Triggers even if user is immune or the stat change is blocked.

#### Justified
**Effect**: +1 Attack when hit by Dark-type move.

#### Weak Armor
**Effect**: -1 Defense, +1 Speed when hit by physical contact move.

**Gen 5 specific**: Only -1 Def and +1 Speed (NOT +2 Speed — changed in Gen 7).

#### Contrary
**Effect**: All stat changes reversed (positive changes become negative, vice versa).

#### Overcoat
**Effect**: Immune to sandstorm and hail damage.

**Gen 5 specific**: Does NOT block powder moves (added in Gen 6).

#### Harvest
**Effect**: 50% chance to restore a consumed Berry at end of turn. 100% chance in sun.

Triggering percentage is per-turn at end of turn.

#### Moody
**Effect**: +2 to random stat, −1 to different random stat each turn.

**Gen 5 specific**: CAN affect accuracy and evasion (Gen 8 removes this ability).

#### Cursed Body
**Effect**: 30% chance to disable attacker's last used move for 4 turns when hit.

#### Illusion
**Effect**: User appears as last Pokémon in party until hit by direct damage.

Breaks on any damaging move, including status moves that deal damage (e.g., Leech Seed, Curse).

#### Imposter
**Effect**: User becomes Transform on switch-in, copying opponent's Pokémon exactly.

Only Ditto has this in Gen 5.

#### Zen Mode
**Effect**: Darmanitan changes form (normal ↔ zen) below 50% max HP at end of turn.

Zen Mode form has different stats: more Def/SpDef, less Atk/SpAtk.

#### Mummy
**Effect**: Contact with this ability changes attacker's ability to Mummy.

#### Unburden (Gen 4 carryover, expanded in Gen 5)
**Effect**: Speed doubles after user's held item is consumed.

**Gen 5 additions**: Triggers on Knock Off, Thief, Air Balloon burst, etc.

#### Telepathy
**Effect**: User is not hit by ally's moves in Double/Triple/Rotation battles.

#### Analytic
**Effect**: 1.3x damage if user moves last in turn.

#### Victory Star
**Effect**: +10% accuracy for user and allies in battle.

Affects all moves used by user and allies.

### Unchanged Gen 3–4 Abilities in Gen 5

All Gen 3–4 abilities that are not overridden remain the same:
- Static, Lightning Rod, Water Absorb, Rough Skin (contact recoil)
- Synchronize, Trace, Iron Fist, etc.

Refer to Gen 3 and Gen 4 specs for those details.

## 6. Double Battles (including Triple and Rotation)

### Double Battles (unchanged from Gen 4)

Two Pokémon per side, each can target any opponent or ally.

Spread moves (hitting both opponents) apply **0.75x damage**.

Single-target moves to a specific opponent deal normal damage.

### Triple Battles (NEW in Gen 5)

**Format**: 3v3, one Pokémon per each of 3 positions: Left, Center, Right.

**Targeting rules**:
- **Left/Right positions**: Can only target Center opponent and adjacent position on opposite side
  - Left can target: Center and Right opponent
  - Right can target: Center and Left opponent
  - Center can target: All three opponents
- **Center position**: Can target all three opponents

**Spread moves**: Still apply 0.75x penalty.

**Abilities**: Field effects apply to all 3 positions.

**Status moves with positioning**: Most moves that affect foes still work normally. Wall moves (Reflect, Light Screen) protect the entire field, not per-position.

### Rotation Battles (NEW in Gen 5)

**Format**: 3v3, but only 1 Pokémon per side is active at a time.

**Rotation mechanic**: User can rotate to a different Pokémon instead of moving (does NOT consume a turn, no priority).

Opponent can also rotate during their turn, creating a different active Pokémon.

**Stat persistence**: Stat changes stay with Pokémon during rotation.

**Ability reactivation**: Abilities that trigger on switch-in do NOT trigger on rotation.

**Held items**: Item effects (e.g., Life Orb damage) persist; consumed items stay consumed.

## 7. Weather System

### Weather Types (unchanged from Gen 4)

Same 5 weather types: **Rain, Sun, Sandstorm, Hail, None**.

### Weather Duration

- **Summoned by move** (e.g., Sunny Day, Rain Dance): 5 turns default, **8 turns with weather rocks** (same as Gen 4)
- **Summoned by ability** (Drizzle, Drought, Sand Stream, Snow Warning): **INDEFINITE** (null turns, no auto-cancel)

Weather from abilities is INDEFINITE in Gen 5 (permanent weather nerf is Gen 6+).

### Weather Effects

#### Rain
- **Thunder accuracy**: 100% (unchanged Gen 2+)
- **Solar Beam**: Requires 2 turns (no immediate execution)
- **Fire-type move damage**: 0.5x
- **Water-type move damage**: 1.5x
- **Damage at end-of-turn**: None (unlike Sandstorm/Hail)

#### Sun / Harsh Sunlight
- **Fire-type move damage**: 1.5x
- **Water-type move damage**: 0.5x
- **Solar Beam**: 1 turn (executes immediately)
- **Growth**: +2 Attack, +2 SpAtk (changed from Gen 4's +1/+1)
- **Damage at end-of-turn**: None

#### Sandstorm
- **Rock-type SpDef**: 1.5x (Gen 4+ feature, unchanged)
- **Rock-type immunity to sandstorm damage**: Yes
- **Damage at end-of-turn**: 1/16 max HP per turn to non-Rock types
- **Accuracy of moves in sandstorm**: No effect (unlike Hail's Thunder accuracy)

#### Hail
- **Blizzard accuracy**: 100% (Gen 4+ feature, unchanged)
- **Ice-type immunity to hail damage**: Yes
- **Damage at end-of-turn**: 1/16 max HP per turn to non-Ice types

#### Weather Rocks

- **Damp Rock** (extends Rain): 8 turns total
- **Heat Rock** (extends Sun): 8 turns total
- **Smooth Rock** (extends Sandstorm): 8 turns total
- **Icy Rock** (extends Hail): 8 turns total

Same as Gen 4 (unchanged).

### Weather Immunity

Abilities that grant weather immunity:

- **Sand Rush, Sand Force, Overcoat**: Immune to Sandstorm damage (NOT hail, rain, or sun)

**Air Balloon**: Ground immunity (not weather-related).

## 8. Status Conditions

### Poison

**Damage**: 1/8 max HP per turn (unchanged Gen 2+)

**Badly Poisoned (Toxic)**: Starts at 1/16, increments to 2/16, 3/16, ..., 8/16 on successive turns. Resets to 1/16 on switch-out.

**Toxic counter behavior in Gen 5** (Showdown confirmed):
- Counter resets on switch
- Does NOT cap at 8/16 (can exceed 8/16 in long battles)
- Removed by Full Heal, Full Restore, Lum Berry, etc.

### Burn

**Damage**: 1/8 max HP per turn (unchanged Gen 2–5, becomes 1/16 in Gen 7)

**Attack reduction**: -50% (halve Attack stat, same as Gen 3+)

**Fire-type immunity**: Fire-type Pokémon are immune to burn (unchanged)

### Paralysis

**Speed reduction**: ×0.25 (unchanged from Gen 3–4; the change to ×0.5 happens in Gen 7)
<!-- VERIFIED Showdown data/mods/gen6/conditions.ts:14: Gen 5 inherits gen6 mod → `spe * 25 / 100` = ×0.25 -->

**Full paralysis chance**: 25% chance to not move (unchanged)

**Electric-type immunity**: Electric-type Pokémon are immune to paralysis from Electric-type MOVES only (not blanket immunity — that's Gen 6)

### Sleep

**Duration**: 1–3 turns (CHANGED from Gen 3's 2–5 and Gen 4's 1–5)

Sleep is applied as a counter. Each turn while asleep:
1. Decrement counter
2. When counter reaches 0, wake up

**Sleep counter reset**: Resets to 1–3 on switch-in (woken up automatically when switched out).

**Wake-up moves**: Wake-up items (Full Awake, Awakening, Full Heal, Full Restore) remove sleep.

### Freeze

**Effect**: Pokémon is frozen solid and cannot move.

**Thaw chance**: 20% (1/5) per turn at start of turn, checked before move execution. Showdown: `this.randomChance(1, 5)` in `data/conditions.ts`.

**Other cures**: Fire-type moves hitting the frozen Pokémon, moves with `defrost` flag (Flame Wheel, Sacred Fire, Flare Blitz, Scald, Steam Eruption), Full Heal, Full Restore, Lum Berry.

**Default immunity**: Ice-type Pokémon are immune to being frozen.

### Confusion

**Duration**: 2–5 turns (unchanged from Gen 3–4)

Displayed as "confused" for 2, 3, 4, or 5 turns remaining.

**Self-hit rate**: 50% chance to hit self each turn with 40 base power physical damage.

**Cure**: Refresh, Full Heal, Full Restore, Lum Berry, or switching out.

### Leech Seed

**Effect**: Drains 1/8 of opponent's max HP per turn; user heals same amount.

**Immunity**: Grass-type Pokémon are immune.

**Cure**: Switching out removes Leech Seed.

## 9. Items

### Held Items Returning from Gen 4

- **Life Orb**: 1.3x damage, 1/10 recoil
- **Choice Scarf**: 1.5x Speed, locks move choice
- **Choice Specs**: 1.5x SpAtk, locks move choice
- **Choice Band**: 1.5x Attack, locks move choice
- **Focus Sash**: Survives 1-HP lethal hit once per battle

### NEW Gems (Gen 5 only)

**All 17 types have Gems**:

- Fire Gem, Water Gem, Electric Gem, Grass Gem, Ice Gem, Fighting Gem, Poison Gem, Ground Gem, Flying Gem, Psychic Gem, Bug Gem, Rock Gem, Ghost Gem, Dragon Gem, Dark Gem, Steel Gem, Normal Gem

**Effect**: 1.5x damage to move of matching type; single-use, consumed before move hits.

Consumed after move executes (or is used by Sheer Force).

### NEW Eviolite

**Effect**: 1.5x Def and SpDef for Pokémon that are not fully evolved (do not have a next evolution stage).

Full evolution determined by Pokédex data (e.g., Charizard is fully evolved; Charmeleon is not).

**Interaction with held item slots**: Takes up the held item slot; cannot be equipped on fully evolved Pokémon in most competitive contexts (item is unusable).

### NEW Rocky Helmet

**Effect**: 1/6 max HP damage to attacker on contact.

### NEW Air Balloon

**Effect**: Grants Ground-type immunity. Pops when holder is hit by ANY damaging move (including special moves).

After popping, immunity is gone and holder is susceptible to Ground-type moves.

**Contact definition**: Explosion and other non-contact moves do NOT pop Air Balloon.

### NEW Red Card

**Effect**: Forces opponent to switch out when holder is hit; single use, consumed.

**Interaction**: Does NOT affect Abilities (e.g., Dragon Tail from opponent still works).

### NEW Eject Button

**Effect**: Holder switches out when hit by opponent's move; single use, consumed.

### NEW Ring Target

**Effect**: Removes type immunities from holder.

Example: Normal-type Pokémon with Ring Target is hit by Ghost-type moves normally (not immune).

**Interaction**: All type immunities are removed, including Ability-granted immunities (but this interaction is limited in Gen 5).

### NEW Absorb Bulb

**Effect**: +1 SpAtk when hit by Water-type move; single use, consumed.

### NEW Cell Battery

**Effect**: +1 Attack when hit by Electric-type move; single use, consumed.

### Leftovers (Gen 1 carryover)

**Effect**: 1/16 max HP healing per turn (unchanged Gen 2+)

### Black Sludge

**Effect**: 1/16 max HP healing per turn for Poison-type Pokémon; 1/8 max HP damage per turn for non-Poison types.

### Exp. Share

**Effect**: Holder receives 50% of battle EXP earned in battle (was 0% in Gen 3–4, changed to 50% in Gen 5).

## 10. Priority System

### Priority Tiers (Gen 5)

Higher positive priority moves execute first. Ties broken by Speed stat.

| Priority | Moves |
|----------|-------|
| +5 | Helping Hand |
| +4 | Magic Coat, Snatch |
| +3 | Detect, Endure, Follow Me, Protect, Quick Guard, Wide Guard |
| +2 | ExtremeSpeed, Feint |
| +1 | Aqua Jet, Bullet Punch, Ice Shard, Mach Punch, Quick Attack, Shadow Sneak, Sucker Punch, Vacuum Wave |
| 0 | Most moves |
| −1 | Vital Throw |
| −3 | Focus Punch |
| −4 | Avalanche, Revenge |
| −5 | Counter, Mirror Coat |
| −6 | Dragon Tail, Circle Throw, Roar, Whirlwind |
| −7 | Trick Room, Magic Room, Wonder Room |

### Key Changes from Gen 4

- **ExtremeSpeed**: Priority +2 (changed from Gen 4's +1)
- **Quick Guard, Wide Guard**: New in Gen 5, priority +3
- **Magic Room, Wonder Room**: New in Gen 5, priority −7 (same as Trick Room)

### Prankster Priority

Prankster grants +1 priority to status moves (non-damaging moves that affect battle state).

**Example**: Thunder Wave becomes priority +1 if used by Prankster Pokémon.

Prankster CANNOT raise priority of damaging moves (e.g., Aqua Jet stays +1, not +2).

## 11. Accuracy & Evasion

### Accuracy Stat Modifiers

**Accuracy stages**: −6 (41%), −5 (50%), −4 (60%), −3 (75%), −2 (80%), −1 (89%), 0 (100%), +1 (113%), +2 (125%), +3 (143%), +4 (167%), +5 (200%), +6 (267%)

Calculated as: `modifier = (3 + stage) / 3` for stage >= 0, else `3 / (3 - stage)`

### Evasion Stat Modifiers

**Evasion stages**: −6 (1.67x), −5 (1.40x), −4 (1.20x), −3 (1.00x, normal accuracy), −2 (0.83x), −1 (0.67x), 0 (1.0x), +1 (0.80x), +2 (0.67x), +3 (0.57x), +4 (0.50x), +5 (0.43x), +6 (0.40x)

Final accuracy = base accuracy × accuracy modifier ÷ evasion modifier

### Accuracy Moves

- **100% accuracy**: Most moves (e.g., Tackle, Thunderbolt)
- **Never misses**: Swift, Aura Sphere (100% accuracy)
- **Variable accuracy**: Thunder (70% → 100% in rain), Blizzard (100% in hail, 70% otherwise)

### Move Accuracy vs Stat Accuracy

Gen 5 distinguishes between:
- **Move accuracy**: Inherent accuracy of the move (e.g., Thunder is 70%)
- **Stat accuracy**: Accuracy/evasion modifiers from stat changes

Both multiply together: `final accuracy = move accuracy × accuracy stat modifier × (1 / evasion stat modifier)`

## 12. Entry Hazards

### Spikes

**Layers**: 1–3 layers max

| Layers | Damage per switch-in |
|--------|-----|
| 1 | 1/8 max HP |
| 2 | 1/6 max HP |
| 3 | 1/4 max HP |

**Immunity**: Flying-type Pokémon are immune.

**Removal**: Defog, Rapid Spin, Switching to a user of these moves.

### Stealth Rock

**Damage**: 1/8 × type effectiveness against the switching Pokémon

Calculated as: `damage = 0.125 × 2^(2 - typeEffect)` for each weakness/resistance.

Effective against all types; damage scales with Pokémon's type weaknesses.

**Immunity**: None (unlike Spikes/Toxic Spikes)

**Removal**: Defog, Rapid Spin.

### Toxic Spikes

**Layers**: 1–2 layers max

| Layers | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| 1 | Poisons switching Pokémon (regular poison) |
| 2 | Poisons switching Pokémon (badly poisoned / Toxic) |

**Poison counter**: Badly poisoned counter starts at 1/16 on switch-in.

**Immunity**: Poison-type Pokémon are immune.

**Removal**: Defog, Rapid Spin.

### Entry Hazard Moves

- **Spikes**: Layers increase by 1 (max 3)
- **Stealth Rock**: Sets Stealth Rock (single layer, no stacking)
- **Toxic Spikes**: Layers increase by 1 (max 2)

## 13. Protect & Detect

### Protect and Detect Mechanics (Gen 5 unchanged from Gen 4)

Protect and Detect block ALL incoming moves in the same turn (priority +3).

**Consecutive use penalty**: Each successive use without interruption halves the success rate:
- 1st use: 100%
- 2nd consecutive: 1/2 (50%)
- 3rd consecutive: 1/4 (25%)
- 4th consecutive: 1/8 (12.5%)
- 5th consecutive: 1/16 (6.25%)
- ...continues halving to 1/256, then effectively 0

**Note**: Gen 5 does NOT cap at 12.5% like Gen 4. The counter starts at 2 and doubles each use (2→4→8→16→32→64→128→256), with success = 1/counter.

**Reset**: Any other move resets the counter.

<!-- VERIFIED Showdown data/mods/gen5/conditions.ts:24-46: stall counter starts at 2, doubles via `counter *= 2` up to counterMax 256, then `randomChance(1, 2^32)`. -->

### New Gen 5 Protect Variants

**Quick Guard** (priority +3): Blocks priority moves (priority > 0) targeting user's side in double/triple battles.

Example: Aqua Jet, Bullet Punch, ExtremeSpeed are blocked. Regular moves are not.

**Wide Guard** (priority +3): Blocks spread moves (Earthquake, Explosion, Surf in doubles) targeting user's side.

Both Quick Guard and Wide Guard use the same consecutive-use penalty as Protect.

## 16. Experience System

### NEW EXP Formula (Gen 5)

The experience formula is COMPLETELY REWRITTEN in Gen 5.

**Base formula**:
```
a = 2 * faintedLevel + 10
b = faintedLevel + userLevel + 10

baseEXP = floor(floor(sqrt(a) * a * a) * defeatedBaseExp / floor(sqrt(b) * b * b)) + 1
```

Where:
- **faintedLevel**: Level of the defeated Pokémon
- **userLevel**: Level of the Pokémon that defeated the opponent
- **defeatedBaseExp**: Base EXP value of the defeated Pokémon species

### Trainer Bonus

**Trainer Pokémon** (not caught by user): ×1.5 multiplier

**User's own Pokémon**: ×1.0 (no bonus)

### Trade Bonus

**Traded Pokémon** (obtained via trade): ×1.5 multiplier (stacks with trainer bonus)

**Special trade bonus**: Pokémon traded with affection ("happiness") get ×1.7 instead (in-game feature, not always used)

**Normal Pokémon**: ×1.0 (no trade bonus)

### Lucky Egg

**Held item**: ×1.5 multiplier to EXP gained

### Exp. Share Mechanic (Gen 5 change)

**Participants in battle**: All Pokémon that dealt damage, were switched in, or used support moves gain EXP.

**Exp. Share holder**: Receives 50% of the awarded EXP (changed from 0% in Gen 3–4).

**Non-participants**: Pokémon not sent in get 0% EXP.

### Example Verification

**Level 5 Rattata vs Level 5 Pidgeot**:
```
a = 2 * 5 + 10 = 20
b = 5 + 5 + 10 = 20
sqrt(a) = sqrt(20) ≈ 4.47
sqrt(b) = sqrt(20) ≈ 4.47

baseEXP = floor(floor(4.47 * 20 * 20) * 58 / floor(4.47 * 20 * 20)) + 1
        = floor(floor(1788) * 58 / floor(1788)) + 1
        = floor(1788 * 58 / 1788) + 1
        = floor(58) + 1
        = 59
```

Varies based on level difference and base EXP.

## 17. End-of-Turn Order

### Sequence

Each turn resolves in this order:

1. **TURN_START phase**: Entry abilities, Trick Room countdown
2. **Action Selection & Priority Sorting**: Players choose moves; ties broken by Speed
3. **TURN_RESOLVE phase**:
   - Accuracy check (move misses or not)
   - Move execution
   - Damage calculation
   - Secondary effects (e.g., burn, paralysis, stat changes)
   - Ability triggers (on hit, on use, etc.)
4. **TURN_END phase**:
   - Ability triggers (e.g., Regenerator switches out, Harvest restores Berry)
   - Weather damage (Sandstorm, Hail: 1/16 damage to non-immune types)
   - Leech Seed drain
   - Burn/Poison damage (1/8 each)
   - Toxic counter increment (1/16, 2/16, ... per turn)
   - Rocky Helmet damage (if holder got hit by contact move)
5. **FAINT_CHECK phase**:
   - Fainted Pokémon are removed
   - If all on one side fainted, battle ends
   - Otherwise, continue to next turn

### Weather and Leech Seed Timing

Weather damage and Leech Seed drain resolve at the TURN_END phase, after move execution, secondary effects, and ability triggers.

**Leech Seed drainage counts as damage**: Triggers Rocky Helmet, Regenerator, etc.
